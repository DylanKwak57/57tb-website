## Design Context

### Users
57 Total Beauty serves Thai customers first, especially women in their twenties through forties who want Korean hair services and products, plus Korean residents and international visitors in Bangkok. Product pages must help customers and salon professionals understand what each product does, which hair condition it suits, and how it is used without creating language or technical ambiguity.

### Brand Personality
Professional, warm, and trustworthy. The experience should communicate premium Korean salon expertise with Thai hospitality, clear explanations, and confidence without exaggerated claims.

### Aesthetic Direction
Refined minimalism using the established Warm Brown palette: warm beige surfaces, dark brown type, muted gold and champagne accents, clean product imagery, and generous editorial spacing. Thai is the primary customer language and must never inherit Latin-only display typography or awkward word-breaking behavior. Light is the default theme with a restrained classic dark alternative.

### Design Principles
- Prioritize Thai readability: use a Thai-capable font, intentional phrase-level line breaks, and comfortable line height before display scale.
- Keep product information direct and evidence-based so customers can distinguish product roles quickly.
- Preserve the warm premium system with restrained color, strong contrast, and uncluttered spacing.
- Adapt layouts by language and viewport instead of mechanically shrinking desktop typography.
- Maintain accessible contrast, zoom behavior, reduced-motion support, and clear keyboard interaction.
